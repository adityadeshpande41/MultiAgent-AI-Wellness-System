# General Health Guidance

1. Regular physical activity reduces risk of cardiovascular disease, type 2 diabetes, and depression.
2. Maintain a healthy body weight through balanced diet and consistent exercise.
3. Avoid smoking and limit alcohol intake.
4. Chronic stress can impair immunity and increase inflammation—practice relaxation techniques like breathing exercises or mindfulness.
5. Monitor blood pressure, cholesterol, and blood glucose as recommended by your physician.
6. Stay current with vaccinations and routine checkups.

# Mental Health and Wellness

**Anxiety Management:**
- Practice deep breathing exercises: inhale for 4 counts, hold for 4, exhale for 6
- Regular exercise can significantly reduce anxiety symptoms
- Limit caffeine intake as it can worsen anxiety
- Maintain consistent sleep schedule (7-9 hours nightly)
- Consider mindfulness meditation or progressive muscle relaxation
- Talk to trusted friends, family, or mental health professionals

**Stress Management:**
- Identify stress triggers and develop coping strategies
- Take regular breaks during work or study
- Practice time management and prioritization
- Engage in hobbies and activities you enjoy
- Consider journaling to process thoughts and emotions

**Sleep Hygiene:**
- Maintain consistent bedtime and wake times
- Create a relaxing bedtime routine
- Avoid screens 1 hour before bed
- Keep bedroom cool, dark, and quiet
- Limit daytime naps to 20-30 minutes

**When to Seek Professional Help:**
- Persistent feelings of sadness, anxiety, or hopelessness
- Difficulty functioning in daily activities
- Thoughts of self-harm or suicide
- Substance abuse concerns
- Significant changes in sleep, appetite, or energy levels

# Common Conditions (Informational)

- **Headaches**: Common causes include dehydration, stress, lack of sleep, eye strain, or tension. Try drinking water, resting in a quiet dark room, applying a cold or warm compress to head/neck, gentle neck stretches, or over-the-counter pain relievers. Seek medical attention for severe, sudden, or persistent headaches.
- **Cold and Flu**: Rest, fluids, balanced diet, and over-the-counter remedies can help relieve symptoms. Seek medical attention if high fever or persistent cough occurs.
- **Muscle Strain**: RICE method—Rest, Ice, Compression, Elevation—helps early recovery.
- **Dehydration**: Symptoms include fatigue, dizziness, and dark urine. Rehydrate with water or electrolyte beverages.
- **Fever**: A temperature above 38°C (100.4°F). Rest and fluids; contact a healthcare provider if it persists or spikes.
- **Minor Injuries**: Clean wounds with mild soap and water; apply antiseptic and keep covered.
